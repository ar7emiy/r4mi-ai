==== SECTION 9.7 — TREE REMOVAL AND REPLACEMENT ====
Zone applicability: All residential and mixed-use zones

The removal of any tree with a trunk diameter exceeding six inches (6") measured
at 4.5 feet above grade requires a Tree Removal Permit. Applicants must demonstrate
that the tree presents a hazard, is diseased beyond treatment, or is required for
an approved development. All removed trees must be replaced at a minimum 1:1 ratio
(one replacement tree for each tree removed) with a species approved by the Urban
Forestry Division. Replacement trees must be a minimum of 2-inch caliper at planting.

Replacement exceptions:
  Hazard tree with written arborist report: replacement may be reduced with discretionary review
  Heritage tree (trunk > 24"): requires Planning Commission approval before removal

constraint_value: 1:1
Reference: Municipal Code §9.7, effective 2021-04-01


==== SECTION 14.3 — RESIDENTIAL FENCING STANDARDS ====
Zone applicability: R-1, R-2, R-3

In zones classified R-1 through R-3, fencing along rear and side property lines
shall not exceed six feet (6') in height without variance approval from the
Planning Commission. Front yard fencing is limited to four feet (4'). Fences
constructed of natural wood, vinyl, or decorative metal are permitted. Chain-link
fencing is prohibited in front yards within all residential zones.

Maximum Heights by Zone:
  R-1: Rear/side 6ft | Front 4ft
  R-2: Rear/side 6ft | Front 4ft
  R-3: Rear/side 6ft | Front 4ft

Variance threshold: any fence exceeding zone maximum requires Planning Commission approval.
constraint_value: 6 ft
Reference: Municipal Code §14.3, effective 2019-01-01


==== SECTION 16.2 — DECK AND PATIO CONSTRUCTION ====
Zone applicability: R-1, R-2, R-3

Decks and patios attached to a primary residential structure are permitted by right
provided they do not exceed thirty inches (30") in height above finished grade at
any point. Decks exceeding 30 inches in height require a building permit and must
meet setback requirements for the applicable zone. Rooftop decks are prohibited
in R-1 zones. All decks must maintain a minimum 5-foot setback from side and rear
property lines.

Height measurement: vertical distance from finished grade to top of decking surface
Permit threshold: >30 in above grade requires structural review
constraint_value: 30 in
Reference: Municipal Code §16.2, effective 2018-07-01


==== SECTION 18.4 — HOME OCCUPATION STANDARDS ====
Zone applicability: R-1, R-2, R-3

Home occupations (business activities conducted from a residential dwelling) are
permitted in residential zones subject to the following conditions: the occupation
shall be clearly incidental and secondary to the residential use; no exterior
evidence of the occupation shall be visible from the public right-of-way; no
customers or clients shall visit the dwelling more than 6 times per day; no
employees other than residents of the dwelling unit shall work on-site.

Permitted uses in R-2: licensed professional services, tutoring, consulting,
telecommuting, crafts and personal services with appointment-only clients.
Prohibited uses: retail sales from premises, food preparation for commercial
distribution, vehicle repair, any use generating excessive noise or traffic.

constraint_value: Allowed
Reference: Municipal Code §18.4, effective 2020-01-01


==== SECTION 22.1 — SOLAR ENERGY SYSTEM STANDARDS ====
Zone applicability: All zones

Residential photovoltaic (PV) solar energy systems are permitted by right in all
residential zones provided system capacity does not exceed twenty kilowatts (20 kW)
AC output for single-family residential properties. Systems within the 20 kW cap
require only a building/electrical permit — no discretionary planning approval.
Systems exceeding 20 kW require Conditional Use Permit review.

Rooftop systems must maintain a minimum 18-inch setback from all roof edges.
Ground-mounted systems in residential zones shall not exceed 8 feet in height
and must meet standard accessory structure setbacks.

System cap for single-family residential: 20 kW AC
constraint_value: 20 kW
Reference: Municipal Code §22.1, effective 2023-01-01


==== SECTION 22.7 — COMMERCIAL SIGNAGE STANDARDS ====
Zone applicability: C-1, C-2

In C-1 and C-2 zones, total sign area for any single business shall not exceed
one square foot of sign area per linear foot of building frontage, up to a maximum
of fifty (50) square feet. Illuminated signs are permitted in C-1 zones provided
they do not exceed 800 lumens of external illumination facing the public right-of-way.
Roof signs and rotating signs are prohibited in all commercial zones.

Formula: max_sign_area = min(building_frontage_ft, 50)
Units: square feet
constraint_value: 32 sq ft
Reference: Municipal Code §22.7, effective 2020-06-01


==== SECTION 31.2 — DEMOLITION REQUIREMENTS ====
Zone applicability: All zones

All structures constructed prior to January 1, 1980 shall require a certified
asbestos and lead paint survey conducted by a licensed environmental assessor
prior to issuance of a demolition permit. Survey results must be submitted with
the permit application. Open code enforcement violations must be resolved or
a compliance agreement executed prior to permit issuance.

Pre-1980 trigger: year_built < 1980 → asbestos survey required
Violation rule: open_violations > 0 → compliance agreement required before permit
constraint_value: Required
Reference: Municipal Code §31.2, effective 2015-03-15


==== SECTION 8.4 — ACCESSORY DWELLING UNITS ====
Zone applicability: R-2, R-3 (per SB-9 2023 update)

Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) are permitted in R-2 and R-3 zones subject to
utility capacity confirmation. Applicants must demonstrate sufficient sewer and
water capacity as measured in Equivalent Dwelling Units (EDU). The binding
capacity figure is the lower of available sewer EDU and available water EDU.
Minimum available EDU required: 1.

ADU size limits:
  Detached ADU: maximum 1,200 sq ft or 50% of primary dwelling, whichever is less
  Attached ADU: maximum 50% of primary dwelling area
  Junior ADU: maximum 500 sq ft within existing structure

constraint_value: 800 sq ft
Reference: Municipal Code §8.4, as amended per SB-9 (2023)


==== SECTION 19.1 — SHORT-TERM RENTAL REGISTRATION ====
Zone applicability: R-1, R-2, R-3 (owner-occupied only)

Short-term rental (STR) permits are available exclusively to owner-occupants.
The applicant name must match the recorded property owner on file with the
County Assessor's Office. Rental of non-owner-occupied properties as short-term
rentals is prohibited and subject to $500 annual surcharge if discovered post-permit.

Owner-occupancy verification: applicant name fuzzy-match against owner registry
Fee calculation: based on bedroom count per current fee schedule
Annual renewal required.

constraint_value: 180 nights
Reference: Municipal Code §19.1, effective 2022-09-01
