==== SECTION 14.3 — RESIDENTIAL FENCING STANDARDS ====
Zone applicability: R-1, R-2, R-3

In zones classified R-1 through R-3, fencing along rear and side property lines
shall not exceed six feet (6') in height without variance approval from the
Planning Commission. Front yard fencing is limited to four feet (4'). Fences
constructed of natural wood, vinyl, or decorative metal are permitted. Chain-link
fencing is prohibited in front yards within all residential zones.

Maximum Heights by Zone:
  R-1: Rear/side 6ft | Front 4ft
  R-2: Rear/side 6ft | Front 4ft
  R-3: Rear/side 6ft | Front 4ft

Variance threshold: any fence exceeding zone maximum requires Planning Commission approval.
Reference: Municipal Code §14.3, effective 2019-01-01


==== SECTION 22.7 — COMMERCIAL SIGNAGE STANDARDS ====
Zone applicability: C-1, C-2

In C-1 and C-2 zones, total sign area for any single business shall not exceed
one square foot of sign area per linear foot of building frontage, up to a maximum
of fifty (50) square feet. Illuminated signs are permitted in C-1 zones provided
they do not exceed 800 lumens of external illumination facing the public right-of-way.
Roof signs and rotating signs are prohibited in all commercial zones.

Formula: max_sign_area = min(building_frontage_ft, 50)
Units: square feet
Reference: Municipal Code §22.7, effective 2020-06-01


==== SECTION 31.2 — DEMOLITION REQUIREMENTS ====
Zone applicability: All zones

All structures constructed prior to January 1, 1980 shall require a certified
asbestos and lead paint survey conducted by a licensed environmental assessor
prior to issuance of a demolition permit. Survey results must be submitted with
the permit application. Open code enforcement violations must be resolved or
a compliance agreement executed prior to permit issuance.

Pre-1980 trigger: year_built < 1980 → asbestos survey required
Violation rule: open_violations > 0 → compliance agreement required before permit
Reference: Municipal Code §31.2, effective 2015-03-15


==== SECTION 8.4 — ACCESSORY DWELLING UNITS ====
Zone applicability: R-2, R-3 (per SB-9 2023 update)

Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) are permitted in R-2 and R-3 zones subject to
utility capacity confirmation. Applicants must demonstrate sufficient sewer and
water capacity as measured in Equivalent Dwelling Units (EDU). The binding
capacity figure is the lower of available sewer EDU and available water EDU.
Minimum available EDU required: 1.

ADU size limits:
  Detached ADU: maximum 1,200 sq ft or 50% of primary dwelling, whichever is less
  Attached ADU: maximum 50% of primary dwelling area
  Junior ADU: maximum 500 sq ft within existing structure

Reference: Municipal Code §8.4, as amended per SB-9 (2023)


==== SECTION 19.1 — SHORT-TERM RENTAL REGISTRATION ====
Zone applicability: R-1, R-2, R-3 (owner-occupied only)

Short-term rental (STR) permits are available exclusively to owner-occupants.
The applicant name must match the recorded property owner on file with the
County Assessor's Office. Rental of non-owner-occupied properties as short-term
rentals is prohibited and subject to $500 annual surcharge if discovered post-permit.

Owner-occupancy verification: applicant name fuzzy-match against owner registry
Fee calculation: based on bedroom count per current fee schedule
Annual renewal required.

Reference: Municipal Code §19.1, effective 2022-09-01
